How to eat more vegetables

Pete Davis

These are my 5 hacks to get more veggies in your diet. The fifth one is a game changer, but I'll start with the reason Veghacker exists...

1. Keep a veg log

"What we measure, we improve" - James Clear, author of Atomic Habits

When you want to create a healthy habit, it helps to measure the right thing. With veg, it's the variety that counts.

I've been keeping a daily log of the different types of veg I eat since 2nd September 2020 and it's really motivating. I made the Veghacker app to make this super convenient.

2. Buy more vegetables

Might seem obvious, but to eat more veg, you must buy more veg.

Next time you are buying groceries, get at least one vegetable you don't normally buy. Doesn't matter if you don't know what to do with it, you can Google once you've got it home.

3. Keep your veggies where you can see them

Is your veg hiding in the drawer at the bottom of the fridge or at the back of a cupboard?

Go to the kitchen now and move all the veg to the front of your shelves, as close as you can to eye level. Any veg that doesn't need to be cold or dark can be kept out in full view.

4. Sneak extra veg into your favorite meals

Every time you make a sandwich, add a slice of cucumber, tomato or a letuce leaf. Every time you make bolognese, grate in some carrot or courgette.

For each of your go-to meals are, think of an extra vegetable you can add.

5. Start at breakfast

Veg should be part of every meal, not just dinner.

Start each day the way you mean to go on by adding some veg to your breakfast. I'm a fan of chopped spring onions in my scrambled eggs!

It's so satisfying to have something to log in the Veghacker app before the day has even got going!

Keep on veghacking

If you try buying different veg, keep it visible and find ways to add veg to meals, even breakfast, you are going to see some wonderful results in your veg log and you'll be making long-term improvements to your health.
